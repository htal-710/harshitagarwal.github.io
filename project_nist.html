01. THE INQUIRY

To map the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB), we use superconducting sensors (MKIDs) that operate near absolute zero. But there was a problem.


The Hypothesis: In the previous paradigm, calibration light was coupled from outside the cryostat, introducing unknown errors. I proposed that moving the entire calibration assembly‚Äîsource, filters, and motion‚Äîinside the dilution refrigerator would eliminate these errors.

02. THE BUILD

Standard mechanics freeze at 4 Kelvin. I engineered a custom Cryogenic Rotary System anchored to the 4K stage.


I utilized SolidWorks to design a mount for a UHV stepper motor and Finite Element Analysis to model thermal loads. The system was automated using Python scripts to sweep through polarization angles autonomously.

03. FIELD NOTES

FAILURE LOG: The thermal battle.

Initially, the cryostat failed to condense due to heat leak from the wiring harness. Later, the motor seized due to thermal contraction. We had to realign the drive rod and adjust thermalization to make it spin at 4K.

04. VALIDATION

The system worked. We successfully rotated the polarized source in-situ. The observed data matched simulations, allowing us to measure the Cross-Polarization Component with high precision.
